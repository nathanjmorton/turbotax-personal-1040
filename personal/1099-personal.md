# Intuit Turbotax Personal Income Tax 1040

- 4 versions:
1. 1040
2. 1040-SR (senior)
3. 1040-NR (non citizen)
4. 1040-X (amendment)

- determine adjusted gross income (AGI) including income and allowable (above the line) deductions
- AGI line 11
- can reduce AGI with standard or itemized deduction expenses including but not limited to:
  - mortgage interest
  - state and local income tax or sales tax
  - charity
  - medical more than 7.5% of AGI

### Recent Updates to 1040
- standard deduction  
  - single 14.6k
  - married 29.2k
  - head of house 21.9k
- form 8812
  - advanced child tax credit 1.7k
- clean vehicle credit form 8936 and schedule 3
- schedule 3
  - home efficient clean energy and home improvement credits on separate lines
  - prev owned cleaned vehicles from form 8936
  - elective payment amt from form 3800
- self employed health insurance deduction 
  - If you can take the self-employed health insurance deduction on Schedule 1, line 17,  and you can't use the Self-Employed Health Insurance Deduction Worksheet in these  instructions, you will now use Form 7206, instead of Pub. 535, to figure your  deduction. 
- charitable one-time deduction 53k
- rmd age updated from 72 to 73
- public safety officer health insurance premiums 3k from distributions
- exception to 10% tax for early distributions
  - federal disasters
  - terminally ill
  - firefighters meeting age or years of service requirement
- direct file pilot for 2024 tax year

### Schedules on 1040
- schedule 1
  - add'l income/deductions eg alimony, cap gains/losses, unemployment, educator expenses, hsa contributions
 - schedule 2
   - add'l taxes eg amt, excess credits for health insurance
   - self employment taxes, unreported soc sec and medicare tax, add'l tax on ira, household employment tax, repayment of first time homebuyer credit, and foreign corps
- schedule 3
  - add'l credits and payments 
  - part 1: nonrefundable credits eg foreign tax credit, childcare expenses, retirement savings contribution
  - part 2: refundable credits and other payments eg net premium tax credit, excess social security, and tier 1 RRTA tax withheld, deferred amt of net 965 tax liability
- schedule a
  -  itemized deductions incl medical, dental, mortgage interest, state and local taxes, charity, casualty and theft losees from federal disaster
- schedule b
  - interest and dividend income > 1.5k with amounts reported carrying to form 1040 lines 2 and 3
  - under 1.5k amounts don't need schedule b, but are still reported on 1040
- schedule c
  - self employed net p&l calculated and transferred to form 1040 line 3
- schedule d
  - capital gains and losses from investments
- schedule e
  - income and loss for pass-through entities, real estate, trusts, royalties
- schedule f
  - 2 parts for additional credits and payments like schedule 3
- schedule h
  - household worker withholdings of social security and medicare
- schedule j
  - farmers and fisherman average 3 prev yrs of tax
- schedule r
  - credit for elderly and disabled
- schedule eic
  - earned income credit for qualifying child
- schedule se
  - self employment tax for 400 or more in profits
- schedule 8812
  - part 1: non-refundable child tax credit and dependents carried to line 19 of 1040
  - part 2: refundable child tax credit carried to line 28 of 1040
